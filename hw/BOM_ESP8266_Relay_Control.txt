===================================================================================
ASSEMBLY NOTES
===================================================================================

1. VERIFY TRANSISTOR PINOUT
   - TO-92 pinout varies by manufacturer
   - Confirm with datasheet before soldering
   - Flat side typically indicates orientation

2. DIODE POLARITY
   - Cathode (stripe) MUST connect to +12V side
   - Incorrect orientation will damage transistor

3. COMMON GROUND
   - ESP8266 GND and 12V supply GND MUST be connected
   - This is critical for proper operation

4. POWER ISOLATION
   - ESP8266 powered via USB (5V)
   - 12V supply only for relay circuit
   - DO NOT connect 12V to ESP8266 VIN

5. RELAY CONTACT WIRING
   - Pin 3 (COM) and Pin 4 (NO) switch your load
   - Observe load voltage/current ratings
   - Use appropriate wire gauge for load current

===================================================================================
TESTING PROCEDURE
===================================================================================

1. Before applying power:
   - Verify all connections with multimeter
   - Check for shorts between power and ground
   - Verify transistor pinout
   - Confirm diode polarity

2. Initial power-up:
   - Connect ESP8266 USB only (no 12V yet)
   - Upload test sketch
   - Verify GPIO output with multimeter

3. Full circuit test:
   - Connect 12V supply
   - Run test program
   - Listen for relay click
   - Measure relay coil voltage
   - Test contact switching with multimeter

4. Load testing:
   - Start with low-current resistive load
   - Gradually increase to rated current
   - Monitor for overheating
   - Verify switching reliability
